Home Robotics

Sensing and Manipulation


Home robotics is as the name says. Itâ€™s having autonomous robots assisting with daily household tasks, like cleaning. Robots rely on their senses just as humans do, yet they tend to have fewer senses than us. This results in robots having to rely more heavily on the senses which they do possess. Arguably, one of the most important senses is vision. Mobile robots have to rely on vision to navigate rooms, while other domestic robots use it to interact with objects.

Without the ability to sense the world around it and process these senses how a would a robot fold your clothes or vacuum the floor? Vision and sensing is critical to home robotics and so this website looks into robots sensing of the physical world around it and how these robots interact with the world

Yet without the optimisation of algorithms and the applications of machine learning to continually improve the robot, these rudimentary senses would never advance past a certain level. It is with these developments in designing and testing a robot that the process of doing their required tasks becomes easier and easier, and the results become evidently better and better.
